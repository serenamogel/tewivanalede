Manny Ramirez | Career

Manny Ramirez was assigned to the Burlington Indians Rookies for his first professional outing.

However, Manny made his pro debut in 1993 against the Minnesota Twins after making an impression during the minor league.

He had a significant impact immediately, hitting several home runs in his debut season. He had had a batting average of.300 by 1994.

In 1995, Ramirez had his best year ever, batting .308 with 31 home runs and 107 RBI.

After the season, he received his first career All-Star Game selection in July and went on to win his first Silver Slugger Award.

He shot two homers in the 1995 American League Championship Series as the Indians advanced to the postseason that year.

On his path to becoming one of the best-run producers in the game, Ramirez’s power skyrocketed in 1998.

He moved from the Cleveland Indians to the Boston Red Sox in 2000 before playing for Los Angeles Dodgers in 2008.
