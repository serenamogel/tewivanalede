Patrice Sanders Biography

Patrice Sanders is an American anchor for FOX45 Morning News who joined the morning news team in April of 2003. She has received numerous awards and community distinctions for her work, including an AP Award for a series tackling the problem of childhood obesity.

In the year 2006, Patrice was awarded Towson University’s Distinguished Alumni of the Year Award. Prior to joining FOX45, she anchored and reported in Boston, California, and Pennsylvania. Patrice’s career in journalism started in 1995 at ABC News Nightline in Washington, DC. Patrice grew up in Mitchellville, Maryland as well as graduated from Towson State University.

She is very active in the community, Patrice frequently speaks at schools, churches, and community events. Much of her time is dedicated to being a mentor for journalism students interested in pursuing a career in news. Patrice has also served as an adjunct teacher for Sheffield Broadcasting Institute, teaching News Writing and Delivery.

She is also passionate about her work with children and giving them a strong foundation for a lifetime of healthy fitness and nutrition habits. Patrice volunteers in city schools to provide fitness camps for students. She also serves as the President of Monte Sanders Fit Families, Inc. The non-profit was founded in 2009 by Sanders and her husband and is aimed at eliminating childhood obesity. Sanders loves reading, photography, and spending time with her husband, and daughter Naomi.
