Ryan Reynolds, a co-star in Deadpool who T.J. Miller called an “insecure person,” is said to “not like” him.

In a recent interview, T.J. Miller spoke candidly about his less-than-ideal working relationship with Ryan Reynolds. The Silicon Valley actor revealed how he believes Reynolds “hates” him during an interview on The Adam Carolla Show. In Deadpool (2016) and Deadpool 2 (2018), the pair collaborated, with Miller portraying Wade Wilson’s closest pal Weasel.

When they said “Cut,” T.J. recalled how he “listened and thought it was weird” and left the stage as a result. Miller also said that the staff showed signs of unease following the scene.

T.J. Miller also hinted that his disagreement with Ryan Reynolds went beyond their on-screen relationship, mockingly remarking that it was the reason the 45-year-old actor behaved in the way he did. He thinks the reason is that he isn’t funnier than Ryan and hasn’t appeared in as many movies as him.

Ryan Reynolds and T.J. Miller will never collaborate again.

No. I wouldn’t collaborate with him once more. Given how talented he is at playing Deadpool, I sort of wish him well because I find it odd that he despises me.”

Miller acknowledged that his opinions about working with Ryan Reynolds might evolve, drawing a comparison to his relationship with Michael Bay, who he is now friends with and would collaborate with again. When the two collaborated in 6 Underground, Bay allegedly had a “very specific impression” of Reynolds.

T.J. Miller is emphatic that he won’t collaborate with Ryan Reynolds and won’t do another Deadpool movie either: “You shouldn’t continue doing something for more than five years, in my opinion. It seems strange to me to play Weasel now, ten years later.”

T.J. Miller reaffirmed that he doesn’t have any bad intentions for Ryan and even brought up Deadpool 3: “They don’t have my bad intentions. I believe [Reynolds] should do a Deadpool 3 and carry on with his filmmaking. I simply believe that he dislikes me, and I found his manner of expressing it to be peculiar.”
